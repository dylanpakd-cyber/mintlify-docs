Proxies give each account its own network identity so platforms don't see many accounts sharing one IP. But you don't always need them — start by deciding whether they apply to your setup.

## Do you actually need a proxy?

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="1–2 accounts per device → no proxy needed" icon="house">
    Your home wifi is perfectly fine. A couple of accounts coming from one home IP looks completely normal, so there's nothing to configure. Skip this page.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Scaling up → yes, use proxies" icon="layer-group">
    Once you're running several devices or stacking more accounts per device, give each account its own IP so platforms don't link them by a shared origin.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  Proxies are optional. If you're not running proxies, just leave Warmr's proxy list empty — accounts go out over your home connection.
</Note>

## Which proxies to use

Proxy quality matters more than price. A cheap proxy on a crowded, recycled IP pool is worse than no proxy at all — you inherit every flag from everyone else who used that IP.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Recommended" icon="circle-check">
    Reputable mobile and residential providers like **AnyIP** and **Bright Data**. Clean pools, large rotation, and IPs that read as genuine mobile or home users.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Avoid" icon="circle-x">
    **IPRoyal** and similar budget providers. Their IPs are heavily shared and recycled, which is one of the fastest ways to land in a shadowban.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Warning>
  Datacenter, shared, and free proxies are well known to platform trust-and-safety systems. They're trivially detected and will raise your flag rate. Stick to clean mobile or residential IPs.
</Warning>

A SIM card per iPhone is also a great option if you have it — each device gets a unique carrier IP with no third-party proxy service at all.

## Set up per-account proxies on the iPhone

Warmr can switch the proxy automatically as it rotates between accounts, so each account always uses its own IP. On the iPhone, you use a proxy client app — **Stash** or **Shadowrocket** (both on the App Store) — and Warmr tells it which server to use.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Add your proxy servers in Warmr">
    Open the **Proxy** section in Warmr and paste in the proxy servers from your provider.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Install Stash or Shadowrocket on the iPhone">
    Install whichever client you prefer from the App Store. Both work the same way with Warmr.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Import the config via QR">
    Warmr's Proxy page shows a QR code (and a **Copy URL** option). In Stash or Shadowrocket, scan the QR to import the config, then turn the VPN toggle on.

    {/* SHOT: Warmr Proxy page QR code next to the iPhone Stash/Shadowrocket import screen */}
  </Step>
  <Step title="Let Warmr rotate automatically">
    From here, Warmr switches the active server itself each time it moves to the next account. You don't touch anything by hand.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Traffic leaves the iPhone directly to your proxy provider — the Mac is never on that path and never sees your account traffic. Keep the iPhone and Mac on the same wifi when you first import a config or add new servers.
</Note>

<Tip>
  Assign each account its own dedicated IP. Reusing one proxy across many accounts re-creates the exact shared-origin pattern proxies are meant to avoid.
</Tip>
